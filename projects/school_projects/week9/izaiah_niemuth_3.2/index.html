Welcome to the Age of Dinosaurs

The world of dinosaurs stretches across millions of years, from the Triassic to the Cretaceous periods, and includes an incredible variety of shapes, sizes, and behaviors. On this site you will explore some of the most famous dinosaurs and learn how they lived, hunted, and survived in a changing world.

Herbivorous dinosaurs such as the long‑necked sauropods spent most of their time searching for plants, while armored dinosaurs like Stegosaurus relied on plates and spikes for protection. Paleontologists study their fossils to learn how these animals moved, ate, and cared for their young.

From towering Tyrannosaurus rex to the swift and agile Velociraptor, each dinosaur had unique adaptations that helped it find food and avoid predators. Fossil evidence shows that some dinosaurs may have lived in groups, migrated together, and even built nests to guard their eggs.

Quick Dino Facts

Some dinosaurs were no bigger than chickens, while others stretched over 30 meters in length. Birds are considered modern descendants of certain dinosaur groups, meaning dinosaurs are not entirely extinct.
