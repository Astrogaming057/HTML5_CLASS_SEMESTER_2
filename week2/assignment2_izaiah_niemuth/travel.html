Key West, Florida

Home | Travel

Key West is a small island city at the southernmost point of the continental United States. It is part of the Florida Keys, a long chain of islands that stretch into the Gulf of Mexico.

The city has a rich history connected to shipping, fishing, and shipwreck salvage. It later became known for writers and artists, and today it blends that history with a relaxed, beach-town atmosphere.

Key West sits about 130 miles southwest of Miami, connected by the scenic Overseas Highway. The drive crosses bridges and open water, which makes the journey part of the experience.

Things to do in Key West include visiting the Southernmost Point marker, snorkeling or boating, and exploring historic homes. I especially liked walking along Duval Street and watching the sunset at Mallory Square, which is beautiful in the evening.
